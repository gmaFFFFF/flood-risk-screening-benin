# Hydrologic Soil Groups (HSG) - USDA NRCS NEH Part 630 Chapter 7

**URL:** https://directives.sc.egov.usda.gov/OpenNonWebContent.aspx?content=48343.wba (Current 2024 Version)
**Date of Access:** 2026-04-12
**Source:** USDA Natural Resources Conservation Service (NRCS), National Engineering Handbook.

## Definitions and Criteria

Hydrologic Soil Groups (HSGs) are used to estimate direct runoff from rainfall. Soils are assigned to one of four groups (A, B, C, D) or three dual classes (A/D, B/D, C/D) based on their minimum infiltration rate and water transmission capacity when thoroughly wet and without vegetation.

### Hydrologic Soil Groups

| Group | Runoff Potential | Infiltration Rate | Typical Soil Characteristics |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **A** | **Low** | High | Deep, well-drained to excessively drained sands or gravelly sands. High water transmission rate. |
| **B** | **Moderate** | Moderate | Moderately deep to deep, moderately well-drained to well-drained soils with moderately fine to moderately coarse textures. |
| **C** | **Slow** | Slow | Soils with a layer that impedes downward water movement or soils with moderately fine to fine textures. |
| **D** | **High** | Very Slow | Clays with high shrink-swell potential, soils with a permanent high water table, or shallow soils over nearly impervious material. |

### Dual Classes (A/D, B/D, C/D)
Dual classes are assigned to soils that are naturally in Group D due to a high water table but can be improved through artificial drainage.
- **First Letter:** The group if the soil is effectively drained.
- **Second Letter (D):** The group if the soil is in its natural (undrained) condition.

### Classification Criteria
The classification is primarily determined by:
1. **Saturated Hydraulic Conductivity ($K_{sat}$):** The rate at which water moves through the most restrictive layer.
2. **Depth to Water Table:** Seasonal high water tables significantly increase runoff potential.
3. **Depth to Restrictive Layer:** Impermeable layers (like bedrock or claypans) near the surface trigger a Group D classification.
